Role Playing Games

Role Playing Games allow for complete customization of your character. Everything from the way they look, to the stats and playstyle as you level up in the game. Below is a standard chart you might see in a video game. Some games require you to pick a class which has a pre-set amount of stats and will determine your abilities in game. For example, if you pick a warrior, you will have higher strength than any other class but have a lower base dexterity. On the left are links to the different powerhouses that have made some of the best RPG's to date.

Class Strength Dexterity Intellect Stamina Play Style
Warrior 5 2 1 4 Melee,Tank
Mage 1 3 5 2 Ranged,Spell Caster
Rouge 2 5 1 3 Melee,Assassin
Shaman 3 2 3 3 Melee,Spell Caster
Hunter 2 4 1 2 Ranged,Gun

While attribute points are static when you choose your class, as you level and progress through the game, you will be awarded with more attribute points to spend on your character. Strength is the character’s brawn; It determines how much physical damage you can inflict on your enemy via swords and axes. Dexterity affects your character’s agility, or how fast you move. Intellect is the primary attribute for classes that use magic. The amount of damage a spell inflicts is dependent on the character’s intellect. Finally, stamina is your overall toughness in the game. How much damage you can absorb before your character dies. For example, the warrior starts with 40 health points and the mage only has 20 health points, allowing the warrior to take more damage.
